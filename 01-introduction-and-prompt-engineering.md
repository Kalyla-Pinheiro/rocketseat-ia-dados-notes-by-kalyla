## Objective

The course aims to move beyond basic usage of **Generative AI**, such as interacting only through web interfaces (e.g., ChatGPT), and advance toward practical applications in **data-related projects**.

## Why Prompt Engineering Matters

In traditional data workflows, it is estimated that around **80% of the work** involves data cleaning and manipulation.

With **Generative AI**, this paradigm changes:
- **A large portion of problems can be solved with a well-written prompt**

The quality of AI outputs depends directly on the **clarity and specificity of the prompt**.

---

## What Is a Prompt?

A **prompt** is the instruction or command provided to a Generative AI model.

Examples:
- “Write a poem about AI” → generic prompt  
- “Create an Instagram post for a coffee shop called Café Aconchego, located in Vila Madalena, announcing a new dessert” → specific prompt  

The more context and detail provided, the better the AI response.

---

## Prompt Engineering

**Prompt Engineering** is defined as the practice of writing effective prompts by structuring clear, complete, and well-directed instructions to achieve more accurate results.

In this lesson, fundamental techniques are presented to help move from a basic to an intermediate level in the use of Generative AI.

---

## Prompt Engineering Techniques

### Role Playing

Assigns a specific role or function to the AI model.

Example:
> “You are a practical and creative event organizer…”

Benefits:
- Provides context  
- Guides AI behavior  
- Improves response accuracy  

---

### Chain of Thought

Used when a task can be solved through **logical steps**.

The AI is guided to follow a reasoning process such as:
1. Identifying the problem  
2. Understanding the main challenge  
3. Proposing a creative solution  

This technique helps the AI:
- Better understand the problem  
- Produce more structured responses  
- Improve performance on complex tasks  

---

### Few-shot (n-shot)

Involves providing **examples within the prompt** to guide the AI.

Example:
- Problem: vegetarian guests  
- Challenge: limited options beyond salad  
- Solution: create a vegetarian food station with diverse options  

Common classifications:
- Zero-shot: no examples  
- One-shot: one example  
- Few-shot / n-shot: multiple examples  

These terms are widely used in **AI model benchmarks**.

---

## Why Are These Terms in English?

Most references, benchmarks, and advancements in Generative AI use English terminology, such as:
- Role Playing  
- Chain of Thought  
- Few-shot  

These concepts commonly appear in model comparisons (e.g., GPT vs Gemini), making familiarity with the original terminology essential.

---

## Key Takeaways

- A well-designed prompt is essential for effective use of Generative AI  
- Simple techniques already enable a transition from basic usage  
- Prompt Engineering is a key skill for data professionals  
- Role Playing, Chain of Thought, and Few-shot form the foundation of effective prompting
